# Copilot in Excel: How to Activate Microsoft’s AI Assistant

Copilot in Excel is a game-changer for spreadsheet users, offering AI-driven insights, formula suggestions, and chart generation. To get started, here’s a direct summary: ensure you have a qualifying Microsoft 365 plan (Business Basic, Standard, Premium, or Enterprise E3/E5), update Excel to version 2402 or later, and then simply click the Copilot button on the Home tab. If you don’t see it, it may be disabled by your IT admin or require activation via File > Options > Add-ins. Once active, you can ask Copilot questions in natural language, like “Show me trends in sales data” or “Create a pivot table.”

# Prerequisites for Copilot in Excel

Before you can turn on Copilot, you must meet these requirements:

  • Subscription: Microsoft 365 Copilot is included with select Business or Enterprise plans. Personal or Family plans are not supported.
  • Excel Version: You need Excel for Windows (Version 2402 or later) or Excel for Mac (Version 16.78 or later).
  • Licensing: Your account must have a Copilot license assigned by your admin.
  • Region: Copilot is rolling out globally, but availability may vary. Check Microsoft’s documentation.
  • Internet: A stable internet connection is required for AI processing.

# Step-by-Step Activation

Follow these steps to turn on Copilot in Excel:

  1. Open Excel and create or open a spreadsheet.
  2. Look for the Copilot icon – it appears as a small sparkle icon on the Home tab, right side of the ribbon.
  3. Click the Copilot button – this opens the Copilot sidebar.
  4. Sign in (if prompted) with your Microsoft 365 work or school account.
  5. Start using Copilot – type a question or command in the chat box at the bottom.

If the Copilot icon is missing:

  • Go to File > Options > Add-ins.
  • At the bottom, select “COM Add-ins” from the Manage dropdown and click Go.
  • Check “Microsoft Copilot (Preview)” and click OK.
  • Restart Excel.

If still missing, contact your IT admin. Copilot may be disabled via the Microsoft 365 admin center under Settings > Org settings > Copilot.

# Troubleshooting Copilot Not Showing Up

Common reasons Copilot doesn’t appear:

  • Wrong subscription: Verify your plan includes Copilot. Check the Microsoft 365 admin portal.
  • Outdated Excel: Run File > Account > Update Options > Update Now.
  • Admin restrictions: Some organizations block Copilot. Ask your admin to enable it.
  • Region lock: If you’re in a region where Copilot hasn’t launched, use a VPN or wait.
  • Disabled add-in: As above, ensure the COM add-in is enabled.

# Using Copilot in Excel – First Steps

Once activated, you can harness Copilot for:

  • Formula help: “Add a column that calculates profit margin.”
  • Data analysis: “Highlight rows where sales exceed $10,000.”
  • Visualization: “Create a bar chart of monthly revenue.”
  • Natural language queries: “What is the average order value?”

Copilot also integrates with existing Excel features like Power Query and PivotTables. For advanced tips, explore our guide on Copilot’s advanced data analysis capabilities .

# Frequently Asked Questions

# Do I need a specific Microsoft 365 subscription to use Copilot in Excel?

Yes. Copilot in Excel requires a Microsoft 365 Business Basic, Business Standard, Business Premium, or Enterprise E3/E5 subscription with a Copilot add-on license. Personal or Family plans do not include Copilot.

# Is Copilot in Excel available on Mac?

Yes, but only on Excel for Mac version 16.78 or later. The activation process is similar: click the Copilot icon on the Home tab after ensuring your subscription and update status.

# Can I turn off Copilot in Excel?

Yes. You can disable Copilot by going to File > Options > Add-ins, unchecking the “Microsoft Copilot (Preview)” COM add-in, and restarting Excel. Alternatively, admins can disable it organization-wide via the admin center.

# Why is Copilot not working even after activation?

Possible reasons: your license is not assigned, you’re offline, or you’re using a non-English language. Check your account settings and try a simple command like “Hello” to test.

# Does Copilot work with Excel Online?

Currently, Copilot in Excel is available only in the desktop app (Windows and Mac). Excel Online support is expected in future updates.
