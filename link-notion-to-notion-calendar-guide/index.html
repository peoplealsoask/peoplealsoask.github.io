Executive Summary: Linking Notion to Notion Calendar synchronizes your databases and tasks into a unified timeline, allowing you to view, edit, and manage events directly from Notion's interface. Here's how to set it up.

# Direct Answer: How to Link Notion to Notion Calendar

Linking Notion to Notion Calendar is straightforward: install the Notion Calendar app (formerly Cron) on your desktop or mobile, sign in with the same Notion account, and grant permission to access your workspace. Once connected, any database with a date property—like tasks, events, or projects—automatically appears as a calendar layer. You can toggle visibility, create new events, and edit details without leaving the calendar interface. This integration turns your Notion workspace into a dynamic time management hub.

# Why Link Notion to Notion Calendar?

Notion excels at organizing information, but its native calendar view lacks the speed and polish of a dedicated calendar app. Notion Calendar fills that gap by offering a lightning-fast, keyboard-friendly interface that syncs bidirectionally. You can:

  • View all your dated items (deadlines, meetings, reminders) in one place.
  • Drag and drop to reschedule tasks directly from the calendar.
  • Use multiple calendar overlays for different projects or contexts.
  • Integrate with Google Calendar, Outlook, and other external calendars.

This connection is essential for anyone using Notion as a productivity system—especially those following GTD, PARA, or second brain methodologies. By bridging your knowledge management and scheduling, you reduce friction and keep your focus on what matters.

# Step-by-Step Setup Guide

# 1. Install Notion Calendar

Visit the Notion Calendar download page and get the app for macOS, Windows, or your mobile device. The mobile version supports iOS and Android. After installation, launch the app and click “Sign in with Notion.”

# 2. Authorize Workspace Access

A browser window will open asking you to allow Notion Calendar to access your Notion workspace. This permission is required to read and write to your databases. Make sure you’re logged into the correct Notion account (the one containing your task databases).

# 3. Link Your Databases

Once authorized, you’ll see an empty calendar. Click the “+” icon in the sidebar or use the shortcut Cmd/Ctrl + K and search for “Add calendar.” Select the Notion workspace and then choose the specific database you want to sync. Every database with at least one date property (e.g., “Date,” “Due,” “Start”) will appear as an option. You can add multiple databases; each becomes a separate, color-coded layer.

# 4. Customize Views and Settings

Click the gear icon next to a calendar layer to rename it, change its color, or filter by properties (e.g., only show tasks tagged “High Priority”). You can also set default event durations and reminder times. Pro tip: create a dedicated “Calendar View” template in Notion that includes a date property and a status property for maximum compatibility.

# Advanced Workflows for Power Users

# Using Linked Databases as Event Sources

Notion Calendar isn’t limited to simple task lists. You can sync:

  • Project timelines – databases with start and end dates become Gantt-like blocks.
  • Content calendars – blog posts, social media drafts, and editorial deadlines.
  • Habit trackers – daily check-ins with a date property show as repeating events.
  • Client schedules – CRM databases with appointment dates.

Each database’s properties (select, multi-select, text) appear as event metadata, so you can filter and search within the calendar.

# Bi-Directional Sync and Real-Time Updates

Changes made in Notion Calendar instantly reflect in your Notion database and vice versa. For example, if you drag a task to tomorrow in the calendar, its date property updates in Notion. This works even if you’re offline—changes sync when you reconnect. However, note that Notion Calendar does not support recurring events natively; you need to duplicate events manually or use a formula to generate recurring dates.

# Integrating with External Calendars

Notion Calendar can overlay Google Calendar, Outlook, and Apple Calendar. Go to Settings > Calendars and click “Add Calendar.” Enter the public iCal URL or connect via OAuth. This allows you to see your work tasks alongside personal appointments. External events are read-only, but you can create Notion events from them using the “Create Notion Event” shortcut.

# Troubleshooting Common Issues

  • Database not appearing? Ensure it has a date property. Also check that the database is shared with the workspace and not restricted.
  • Events not syncing? Refresh the calendar by pressing Cmd/Ctrl + R. If that fails, remove and re-add the database layer.
  • Calendar app crashes? Update to the latest version. For persistent crashes, try resetting the app data via Settings > Advanced > Reset.
  • Permissions errors? Re-authorize the app in your Notion settings under Integrations.

# Frequently Asked Questions

# Can I link multiple Notion databases to one calendar?

Yes. You can add as many databases as you like, each as a separate layer. Use the sidebar to toggle visibility, and color-code layers to distinguish projects or contexts.

# How do I create a new event in Notion from the calendar?

Double-click an empty time slot (or press N on the keyboard) to open the quick-create dialog. Enter a title, choose the target database, and fill in any required properties. The event will appear in both Notion and the calendar.

# Why are my events not showing up on the correct date?

Check your timezone settings in Notion Calendar (Settings > General > Timezone) and ensure your database date property is set to the correct timezone. Notion Calendar respects the timezone of each event.

# Does Notion Calendar support recurring events?

Not directly. Currently, you must create individual events or use a formula in Notion to generate future dates. Some users set up a template with a “repeat” property and copy pages manually.

# Can I use Notion Calendar offline?

Yes. The app caches recent data, so you can view and edit events offline. Changes will sync when you reconnect to the internet.

# The Future of Notion Calendar in 2026

As of 2026, Notion Calendar continues to evolve. Recent updates include AI-powered event suggestions, deeper integration with Notion AI for meeting notes, and improved performance on large databases. Expect better support for recurring events and native email integration in upcoming releases. For now, the link between Notion and Notion Calendar remains one of the most powerful productivity combos available.
