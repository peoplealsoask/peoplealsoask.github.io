Does NASA Use WordPress? The Surprising Truth About Their Tech Stack

Discover if NASA uses WordPress for its websites. Uncover the surprising mix of CMS platforms and why they rarely rely on WordPress.

Executive Summary: NASA does not primarily use WordPress for its official websites. They rely on custom-built CMS and Drupal for security and scale, but some sub-sites may occasionally use WordPress for specific project pages or blogs.

Yes, you read that right. While the popular myth suggests NASA runs on WordPress, the reality is far more complex and security-focused. Let’s dive deep.

# The core answer: NASA’s primary CMS choices

NASA’s main public website (nasa.gov) and most of its sub-sites are powered by Drupal, an open-source content management system known for its robust security features and scalability. They also heavily rely on a custom-built CMS called NASA Web Enterprise Content Management System (WECMS). These platforms provide the granular access controls, audit trails, and compliance with federal standards that WordPress, in its default form, cannot offer without extensive customization.

# Why WordPress is rarely the choice for government agencies

While WordPress powers over 40% of the web, its popularity makes it a constant target for hackers. For an agency like NASA, a breach could be catastrophic. Key reasons they avoid WordPress:

  • Security: WordPress’s plugin ecosystem introduces vulnerabilities. NASA requires FedRAMP-compliant systems.
  • Scale: NASA hosts millions of pages, images, and videos. WordPress can handle this, but enterprise-level hosting and optimization become expensive.
  • Custom workflows: NASA needs custom user roles, approval chains, and integration with legacy databases—features that are easier with Drupal or custom code.

# Does NASA use WordPress anywhere?

Surprisingly, yes—but only for experimental or non-critical projects. Some NASA internal blogs, educational microsites, or hackathons have used WordPress. For example, the NASA Open Innovation team once ran a WordPress site for a public challenge. However, these are isolated cases and never for mission-critical data.

# The tech stack behind NASA’s websites

To fully answer “Is NASA using WordPress?”, we must look at their arsenal:

  • nasa.gov: Drupal 9/10 with heavy custom modules.
  • science.nasa.gov: Custom-built React frontend with headless CMS.
  • Spot the Station: A mobile app and webapp using custom APIs.
  • NASA+ (streaming): Built on AWS with a custom CMS.

They also use GitHub for repositories, Jira for project management, and Confluence for internal wikis. WordPress simply doesn’t fit their enterprise needs.

# What this means for your site

If you’re considering WordPress for a high-security or high-traffic site, study NASA’s reasoning. For 95% of businesses, WordPress is excellent. But for defense-grade security, look at Drupal, Joomla, or headless solutions.

# Frequently Asked Questions

# Is NASA’s website built with WordPress?

No, the main NASA website runs on Drupal, not WordPress. Some legacy or experimental sub-sites may have used WordPress, but it is not their standard.

# Why does NASA prefer Drupal over WordPress?

Drupal offers superior security, granular user permissions, and better performance at scale—critical for a federal agency handling sensitive information.

# Can I build a NASA-like site with WordPress?

Yes, with many plugins and a high-security hosting provider, but you’ll need to invest heavily in security hardening, custom development, and compliance measures.

# Does NASA contribute to WordPress?

No, NASA does not officially contribute to the WordPress core. They have their own open-source initiatives (like their own CMS plugins) but not focused on WordPress.

# What CMS does the White House use?

Like NASA, the White House website also uses Drupal, following the trend of government sites valuing security and control.
