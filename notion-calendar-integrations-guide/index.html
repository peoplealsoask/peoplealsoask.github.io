Executive Summary: Notion integrates natively with Google Calendar, Outlook Calendar, and Apple Calendar via embed, plus third-party tools like Cron and Calendly offer deeper two-way sync. This guide explores all options for 2026.

The question on every productivity enthusiast’s mind: What calendars integrate with Notion? As we move through 2026, Notion has solidified its position as the ultimate all-in-one workspace, but its calendar capabilities remain a hot topic. The good news is that you can sync almost any major calendar with Notion, though the method varies. Below, we break down native integrations, third-party tools, and workarounds to help you choose the best fit for your workflow.

# Native Calendar Integrations: The Basics

Notion doesn’t have a built-in calendar app like Google Calendar, but it offers powerful embedding options. Here’s how the big three stack up:

# Google Calendar

Google Calendar is the most popular choice. You can embed a live view using the /embed command and pasting the public or private iCal link. For two-way sync, look to third-party tools (see below). To embed, go to your Google Calendar settings, copy the secret iCal address, and embed it in Notion. You’ll see events in real-time.

# Outlook Calendar

Outlook Calendar (Microsoft 365) also supports iCal sharing. Similar to Google, generate a published iCal link from Outlook’s settings and embed it. Note: Embeds are read-only; you can’t edit events from Notion directly.

# Apple Calendar

iCloud Calendar users can share calendars via public URL. Copy the .ics link and embed it. This works well for Mac/iOS users, but again, it’s one-way.

# Deep Sync with Third-Party Tools

For two-way sync (add events from Notion to your calendar), you need middleware. Here are the top contenders:

# Cron

Cron, now part of Notion, offers a native-like experience. It syncs with Google Calendar and Outlook, allowing you to view and edit events directly in a Notion-like interface. Cron is especially popular for its inline scheduling and time zone features.

# Calendly

Calendly integrates with Notion via embed and API. You can embed your booking page in a Notion page, but for syncing scheduled events to a calendar, you’ll need the standard Calendly integration with Google/Outlook/Apple.

# Zapier and Make (formerly Integromat)

These automation giants connect Notion with almost any calendar. You can create workflows like: “When a new Notion database item is created, add an event to Google Calendar,” or vice versa. This requires some setup but is highly customizable.

# Step-by-Step: How to Embed a Calendar in Notion

Let’s walk through embedding Google Calendar:

  1. Open Google Calendar → Settings → Settings for my calendars → [calendar name] → Integrate calendar.
  2. Copy the Secret address in iCal format (ends in .ics).
  3. In Notion, type /embed and paste the link.
  4. Resize the embed block to fit your page.

Repeat for Outlook and Apple Calendar using their respective sharing settings.

# Advanced Integration: Notion’s Calendar View

Notion’s own database calendar view (created from a table or board) doesn’t sync with external calendars natively. However, you can use it as a project timeline. To see external events in the same view, consider using the embed method within the calendar page.

# Pros and Cons of Each Method

Method Pros Cons
Embed (iCal) Free, no extra apps Read-only, no two-way sync
Cron Native-feeling UI, supports Google & Outlook Requires download, limited to desktop
Zapier/Make Infinite customization, triggers & actions Subscription cost, learning curve

# Frequently Asked Questions

# H2: Notion Calendar Integration FAQs

# H3: Does Notion sync with Google Calendar?

Yes, you can embed Google Calendar into Notion using an iCal link. For two-way sync, use Cron or Zapier.

# H3: Can I embed Apple Calendar in Notion?

Yes, by sharing your iCloud calendar publicly and copying the .ics link. Note: only read-only.

# H3: Is there a native Notion calendar app?

Notion has a database calendar view, but it doesn’t sync with external calendars. Cron is the closest to a native app.

# H3: How do I sync Outlook Calendar with Notion?

Generate a published iCal link from Outlook’s sharing settings and embed it in Notion. For edit capability, use Cron or Zapier.

# Best Practices for 2026

To maximize productivity, combine Notion’s database calendar with an embedded live view of your external calendar. Use tags and filters to manage events. For team use, consider [Cron or a similar dedicated tool] that offers deeper integration without complex setups.

Remember, the ideal integration depends on your needs: read-only embeds are perfect for reference, while two-way sync is essential for active scheduling. As of 2026, Notion’s ecosystem is richer than ever, so experiment and find what works for you.
