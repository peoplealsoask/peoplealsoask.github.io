Executive Summary: GPTExcel offers a limited free plan with basic features (e.g., up to 10 queries/day, no API). Full access requires a paid subscription starting at $9/month. This guide covers everything.

# TL;DR: Is GPTExcel Free?

Yes, GPTExcel has a free tier, but it comes with restrictions. Free users get up to 10 GPT queries per day, access to basic Excel functions, and limited cell range processing. For unlimited queries, advanced formulas, API access, and priority support, you need a Pro plan ($9/month) or Enterprise plan (custom pricing).

# What Is GPTExcel?

GPTExcel is an AI-powered add-in for Microsoft Excel that integrates OpenAI’s GPT models directly into your spreadsheets. Instead of writing complex formulas or VBA code, you can ask natural language questions like “Calculate the average of column B rows where column A > 10” and GPTExcel generates the formula instantly. It also helps clean data, create charts, and automate repetitive tasks.

# GPTExcel Pricing Breakdown (2026)

# Free Plan

  • Cost: $0
  • Daily Queries: 10 GPT requests
  • Processing Limit: Up to 500 cells per operation
  • Features: Basic formula generation, data cleaning, chart suggestions
  • Support: Community forum only

# Pro Plan

  • Cost: $9/month (or $90/year – save 17%)
  • Daily Queries: Unlimited
  • Processing Limit: Up to 10,000 cells per operation
  • Features: Advanced formulas, custom GPT prompts, API access, priority email support
  • Perfect for: Freelancers, small businesses, power users

# Enterprise Plan

  • Cost: Custom pricing (typically $20+/user/month)
  • Features: Everything in Pro + SSO, dedicated server, custom AI models, advanced security, SLA
  • Best for: Large organizations handling sensitive data

# What Can You Do With GPTExcel Free?

The free tier isn’t just a trial; it’s genuinely useful for casual users. Here are tasks you can accomplish:

  • Generate simple formulas (SUM, AVERAGE, VLOOKUP, IF statements)
  • Clean data – remove duplicates, trim spaces, fix date formats
  • Create basic charts – bar, line, pie (with guided suggestions)
  • Translate text within cells (up to 10 translations/day)
  • Extract insights – e.g., “Show me the top 5 products by sales”

However, heavy users will hit the 10-query cap quickly. If you’re working on large datasets or need real-time automation, the free plan feels restrictive.

# How to Get the Most Out of the Free Plan

  1. Batch your queries – Combine multiple requests into one prompt (e.g., “Clean column A, then average column B”).
  2. Use simple language – Avoid complex multi-step instructions that may waste queries.
  3. Leverage the community – The free forum has templates and pre-built prompts.
  4. Monitor your usage – GPTExcel shows a counter; reset daily.

# Should You Upgrade to Paid?

Upgrade if you:

  • Process more than 500 cells a day
  • Need automation (e.g., weekly reports)
  • Want custom AI prompts for repetitive work
  • Require API access for integration
  • Value priority support

Otherwise, the free plan may suffice for light, occasional use.

# Frequently Asked Questions

# H2: General Questions

# H3: Is GPTExcel free forever?

Yes, the free tier is permanent – no trial period. You keep the 10 queries/day limit indefinitely.

# H3: Can I use GPTExcel offline?

No, GPTExcel requires an internet connection to communicate with OpenAI’s servers. Offline functionality is not available.

# H2: Pricing & Limits

# H3: What happens if I exceed my daily free queries?

You’ll see a message to upgrade or wait until the next day (UTC reset). Your existing data and formulas remain untouched.

# H3: Are there any hidden costs in the free plan?

No, it’s completely free with no credit card required. You only pay if you voluntarily upgrade.

# H2: Features & Security

# H3: Does GPTExcel store my data?

Data is processed in memory and not stored permanently by GPTExcel. However, queries may be sent to OpenAI – check their data policy.

# H3: Can I trust GPTExcel with sensitive business data?

Enterprise plans offer data privacy guarantees, but free/Pro users should avoid uploading confidential information.

# Final Verdict

GPTExcel’s free plan is a generous starting point for Excel users curious about AI assistance. It’s not unlimited, but enough for occasional tasks. For professionals who rely on Excel daily and want to save hours, the Pro plan is a bargain at $9/month. In 2026, GPTExcel remains a top choice, but always compare with evolving alternatives.
