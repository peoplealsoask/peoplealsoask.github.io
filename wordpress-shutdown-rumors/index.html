WordPress Shutdown Rumors: Separating Fact from Fiction

Get the latest on WordPress shutdown rumors and facts

Sophia Taylor June 28, 2026 3 min read
Table of Contents
  • Frequently Asked Questions
    • General Questions
    • Technical Questions
Executive Summary: WordPress is not shutting down, with the platform continuing to evolve and improve under the guidance of its community and Automattic, the company behind WordPress.com.

WordPress is one of the most popular content management systems (CMS) in the world, powering over 40% of all websites on the internet. Given its widespread use, any rumors or speculation about the platform’s future can cause concern among its vast user base. Recently, rumors have been circulating about the potential shutdown of WordPress, leaving many to wonder if their websites are at risk. The short answer to the question of whether WordPress is going to shut down is no. WordPress is an open-source platform, which means that its source code is freely available for anyone to use, modify, and distribute. This open-source nature ensures that WordPress cannot be ‘shut down’ in the classical sense, as it is not a proprietary platform controlled by a single entity. However, the rumors may stem from concerns about the future of WordPress’s commercial arm, WordPress.com, which is run by Automattic. Automattic is a for-profit company that offers a range of services and products related to WordPress, including website hosting, custom themes, and e-commerce solutions.

To understand the relationship between WordPress and Automattic, it’s essential to delve into the history of the platform. WordPress was first released in 2003 by Matt Mullenweg and Mike Little, and it quickly gained popularity as a free and open-source CMS. In 2005, Mullenweg founded Automattic, which began to offer commercial services related to WordPress. Today, Automattic is one of the leading companies in the WordPress ecosystem, with a range of products and services that cater to the needs of WordPress users. While Automattic is a for-profit company, its commitment to the open-source principles of WordPress remains strong. The company continues to contribute to the development of WordPress, with many of its employees actively involved in the WordPress community. For users looking to stay up-to-date with the latest WordPress trends and best practices, checking out the official WordPress documentation is a great place to start. This comprehensive resource provides detailed guides on how to get the most out of WordPress, from installing plugins and themes to optimizing website performance. In conclusion, while rumors about the shutdown of WordPress are unfounded, it’s essential to stay informed about the platform’s development and future plans. By understanding the relationship between WordPress and Automattic, users can better navigate the WordPress ecosystem and make informed decisions about their online presence.

# Frequently Asked Questions

# General Questions

  • What is the future of WordPress? WordPress continues to evolve and improve, with a strong focus on open-source principles and community involvement.
  • Is WordPress.com going to shut down? There are no indications that WordPress.com, the commercial arm of WordPress, is going to shut down. Automattic remains committed to providing high-quality services and products to its users.

# Technical Questions

  • How do I keep my WordPress website secure? Keeping your WordPress website secure involves regular updates, strong passwords, and the use of reputable plugins and themes.
  • What are the best practices for optimizing my WordPress website’s performance? Optimizing your WordPress website’s performance involves using caching plugins, optimizing images, and minimizing HTTP requests.
