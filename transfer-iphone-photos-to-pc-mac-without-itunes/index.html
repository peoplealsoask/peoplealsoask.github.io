How to Transfer iPhone Photos to PC or Mac Without iTunes: The Ultimate 2026 Guide

Learn the fastest ways to move iPhone photos to a computer without iTunes using Windows File Explorer, iCloud, AirDrop, and third-party tools. No cable? No problem.

# Direct Answer: How to Move iPhone Photos to Computer Without iTunes

You do not need iTunes to transfer your iPhone photos to a computer. In 2026, Apple has simplified the process, and third-party tools have become even more reliable. The easiest ways are:

  • For Windows: Connect your iPhone via USB cable, unlock it, and trust the computer. Your iPhone appears as a portable device in File Explorer where you can copy photos directly.
  • For Mac: Use the built-in Image Capture app or Photos app. Connect via cable or use AirDrop for wireless transfers.
  • Wireless options: iCloud Photos syncs automatically across devices, and cloud services like Google Photos, OneDrive, or Dropbox let you upload from iPhone and download on your computer.

Below we dive deep into each method step by step.

# Method 1: Using File Explorer (Windows) or Image Capture (Mac)

# Windows

  1. Connect your iPhone to your PC using a USB cable.
  2. Unlock your iPhone and tap “Trust This Computer” if prompted.
  3. On your PC, open File Explorer and click on your iPhone under “This PC.”
  4. Navigate to Internal Storage > DCIM folder. Your photos are organized in subfolders (e.g., 100APPLE).
  5. Copy and paste or drag-and-drop the files to any folder on your PC.

# Mac

  1. Connect your iPhone via USB.
  2. Open Image Capture (from Applications or Spotlight).
  3. Your iPhone appears in the left sidebar. Select the photos you want and choose a destination folder.
  4. Click Import or Import All.

This method requires a cable but no software installation. It’s fast and reliable for bulk transfers.

# Method 2: iCloud Photos

iCloud Photos keeps your entire library synced across devices. This wireless method is ideal if you have enough iCloud storage (free 5 GB; upgrades available).

# Steps

  1. On your iPhone, go to Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud > Photos.
  2. Enable Sync this iPhone (or iCloud Photos toggle).
  3. On your Windows PC, download and install iCloud for Windows from the Microsoft Store.
  4. Sign in with the same Apple ID and enable Photos.
  5. Open File Explorer and go to iCloud Photos in the sidebar. Downloaded photos appear there.

On a Mac, iCloud Photos is native—open the Photos app and all synced images are available.

Pros: No cables, automatic updates. Cons: Requires storage plan for large libraries.

# Method 3: AirDrop (Mac Only)

AirDrop uses Wi-Fi and Bluetooth to transfer photos wirelessly between iPhone and Mac.

  1. On your Mac, open a Finder window and click AirDrop in the sidebar. Make sure Wi-Fi and Bluetooth are on.
  2. On your iPhone, open the Photos app, select the images you want to transfer.
  3. Tap the Share button (square with arrow) and choose your Mac from the AirDrop list.
  4. Accept the transfer on your Mac. Photos land in the Downloads folder by default.

AirDrop is fast but only works between Apple devices. For PCs, use cloud services or cable .

# Method 4: Third-Party Cloud Apps

Services like Google Photos, OneDrive, and Dropbox offer dedicated iPhone apps that upload photos to the cloud automatically. You can then download them on any computer.

  • Google Photos: Free unlimited storage (compressed) or original quality with Google One. Enable backup in app.
  • OneDrive: 5 GB free. Uploads from iPhone camera roll automatically.
  • Dropbox: 2 GB free. Manual upload or camera upload feature.

# Steps (General)

  1. Install the service’s app on your iPhone.
  2. Sign in and enable camera upload.
  3. Photos sync automatically. On your computer, visit the service’s website or install its desktop app to download the images.

These methods work cross-platform and require internet but no cables.

# Frequently Asked Questions

# Can I use a USB cable to transfer without iTunes?

Yes. On Windows, your iPhone appears as a digital camera in File Explorer. On Mac, use Image Capture or the Photos app. No iTunes needed.

# Why won’t my iPhone photos appear on my computer?

Common reasons: iPhone locked, not trusted, or cable faulty. Make sure to unlock your iPhone and tap “Trust” when prompted. Also check that the DCIM folder is visible.

# How do I transfer photos from iPhone to computer wirelessly without iCloud?

Use AirDrop (Mac) or third-party cloud apps like Google Photos. You can also use apps like Feem or SHAREit for direct Wi-Fi transfers.

# Is it safe to use third-party transfer tools?

Stick to reputable apps from official app stores. Avoid tools requesting excessive permissions. The methods listed above (File Explorer, iCloud, AirDrop, big cloud services) are safe.

# How do I transfer Live Photos without losing animation?

On Windows, copy the .HEIC and .MOV files together from the DCIM folder. On Mac, Image Capture and Photos preserve Live Photos. Cloud services vary—Google Photos keeps them as videos.

By following these methods, you can free up space on your iPhone without ever opening iTunes. Choose the one that best fits your setup.
