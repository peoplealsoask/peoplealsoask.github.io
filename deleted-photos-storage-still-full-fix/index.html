Deleted Photos But Storage Still Full? Here's Why and How to Fix It (2026 Guide)

Is your iPhone or Android storage still full after deleting photos? Learn the real reasons (including Recently Deleted & caches) and step-by-step fixes to reclaim space.

Executive Summary: Even after deleting photos, storage remains full because they often move to a 'Recently Deleted' folder for 30 days, plus caches and other hidden files take up space. To free up storage, permanently delete from Recently Deleted, clear app caches, and check for large files.

Even after you delete photos from your device, your storage may still show as full. This happens because of a few common culprits: the Recently Deleted album, system caches, temporary files, or other large media. Understanding these hidden space hogs is the first step to reclaiming storage.

# Why Storage Is Still Full After Deleting Photos

# The Recently Deleted Folder

On both iOS and Android, photos you delete aren’t instantly erased. They move to a Recently Deleted folder where they remain for 30 days (iPhone) or 60 days (some Android versions) before automatic permanent deletion. During this time, the space is still occupied. Many users forget to manually empty this folder.

# System Caches and Temporary Files

Apps like Instagram, WhatsApp, and browsers store thumbnails, downloads, and cache files that can accumulate gigabytes over time. When you delete photos, these caches remain untouched. On Android, the ‘Cached data’ section can be cleared in settings; on iPhone, you may need to offload apps or clear Safari cache.

# Other Hidden Large Files

Videos, especially in 4K, take up far more space than photos. Also look for duplicate files, voice memos, downloaded files, and system data like iOS’ ‘Other’ storage category. These often go unnoticed but can be massive.

# How to Actually Free Up Storage

# Step 1: Empty Recently Deleted

  • iPhone: Open Photos app, tap Albums, scroll to Recently Deleted, tap Select, then Delete All. Note: This action is permanent.
  • Android: Open Google Photos, tap Library, then Trash, select all, and Delete permanently.

# Step 2: Clear App Caches

  • iPhone: For browsers, go to Settings > Safari > Clear History and Website Data. For other apps, offload and reinstall (Settings > General > iPhone Storage).
  • Android: Go to Settings > Storage > Cached data, tap Clear. Or use app-specific settings.

# Step 3: Check for Duplicates & Large Files

Use a file manager or duplicate cleaner app (like Gemini Photos for iPhone). Also review Downloads folder and delete old downloads. To find large files, use storage analyzers like DiskUsage (Android) or Files by Google.

For a complete walkthrough of clearing iPhone system cache, see our guide to freeing up iOS storage .

# Frequently Asked Questions

# Why does my iPhone say storage full after deleting photos?

The most likely reason is that your deleted photos are still in the Recently Deleted folder. They remain there for 30 days unless manually cleared. Also, system caches and ‘Other’ storage can be large.

# How long do deleted photos stay in Recently Deleted?

On iPhone, 30 days. On Android (Google Photos), 60 days. During that period, they continue to occupy space.

# What other files could be taking up storage?

Common space hogs include: large videos (especially 4K), cached app data, downloaded files, duplicate photos, voice memos, and system logs. Use a storage analyzer to identify them.

# Will clearing caches delete my app data?

Clearing caches usually removes temporary files but not your personal data (like logins or saved content). However, offloading an app on iPhone will delete the app but keep its documents/data.

# How can I prevent storage from filling up again?

Enable iCloud Photos (or Google Photos) to store originals in the cloud, use Optimize Storage on iPhone, regularly empty Recently Deleted, and review large files monthly.
